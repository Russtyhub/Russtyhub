Using an LSTM to Forecast River Ice Breakup
Throughout Alaska

We employed a specialized deep learning model designed for analyzing time series data to forecast the timing of river ice breakup across several major rivers in Alaska, USA, and Western Canada. The model uses relatively few meteorological variables to make accurate predictions, forecasting the breakup date within an average margin of 5.90 days. To test the model's practicality, we evaluated its performance using forecasted data for the breakup season. Impressively, the model maintained its accuracy, making accurate predictions even with inputs projected up to five months in advance. Notably, the model does not require specific location details to make predictions, demonstrating its reliability across 20 diverse locations and successful performance on four out of five holdout locations (locations not used for model training).
